Guided Search - Financial Aid

Journey curation tool instructions

Thank you for helping us work to improve the Search experience for journeys such as "student loan forgiveness". Below you will find more information about this project, and instructions on how to help us deconstruct journeys.

Background

For complex and important topics such as [how to pay for college] or [how to get student loan forgiveness], Google search results are dependent on the student knowing what to search for, and students don't always search effectively. Even when they do, the search results often show a variety of articles on the topic, each one with a different perspective. This can be overwhelming for students and lead to them not getting the information they need. (example search results).

Our goal is to guide the user (student) through these complex topics. To do that, we need to unpack these topics into a set of subtopics (bite-sized pieces). Sometimes these subtopics can be further unpacked to create a hierarchy of subtopics, like a table-of-contents of the topic. We want to focus on these principles:

  • Subtopics for exploration rather than prescriptive steps or instructions, and
  • Understandable more than comprehensive.

To guide the user, for each subtopic, we also want to provide a well-chosen Google search query whose search results provide high-quality information on the subtopic (through web answers, web results, videos, etc). We wish to guide students using just these queries, and let the search pages supply the answers. For a topic like [student loan forgiveness], in a Google doc, we can assemble this example hierarchy of subtopics and search queries, and turn it into a Search experience like this demo. Note the wealth of information on the search pages for each subtopic. This approach extends to other domains: e.g. for a use case like [how to buy a house], we can put together this hierarchy and convert it into this demo.

We would like to ask you to combine your domain expertise with information from the top web results for each topic, to construct such a hierarchy of subtopics and search queries. To make this easier, on the next few pages, we have collected the sections listed in the top results for the query [how to get student loan forgiveness] and other use cases. Please use these as raw material for your subtopic hierarchies. For a bird's-eye view, we have partially collapsed the information, but use the "Expand" link on the top of each page (or the "»" links) to see more details.

Instructions

  • Make a copy of this Google doc and share it with us (sitaram@google.com). This is where you will summarize your ideas at the end of the process.
  • For use cases in this doc that you are familiar with, please go through the following steps:
    1. First take a moment to think about how you would break it down into subtopics, as you might explain to a user seeking advice. Feel free to take scratch notes on your notepad.
    2. Try grouping these points into a hierarchy similar to the ones above (ideally 2-3 levels deep, 3-5 subtopics in each list), which might resemble chapters and sections of a book on this topic.
    3. Then look over the raw material extracted from web results on these pages (hit "next" to go through all the use cases). Look for subtopics you might have missed, or different ways of organizing them, and try to improve your hierarchy.
    4. Transcribe your hierarchy for this use case back into your copy of the Google doc (if you did the work on a notepad).
    5. For each subtopic (at every level of the hierarchy), identify a good search query, and add it after a slash ("/") after the subtopic. For this, try Googling a few search queries that you think are reasonable for that topic, and capture which one seems to have useful information on the search results page. (You don't have to read the webpages).
    6. Proceed to the next use case.
  • Please make sure you shared the doc with sitaram@google.com.

Thank you so much for your time and assistance!
